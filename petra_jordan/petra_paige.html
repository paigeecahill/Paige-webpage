Trip to Petra, Jordan

Petra is a historical and archaeological city in southern Jordan. Petra is more popular for its 800 individual monuments that includes buildings, tombs, baths, funerary halls, temples, arched gateways, and colonnaded streets, that were mostly carved from the sandstone. The city was established in 312 B.C. making it one of the oldest metropolises in the world. Petra lies on a slope in the basin among the mountains which form the eastern flank of Arabah valley that runs from the Dead Sea to the Gulf of Aqaba.

Petra has to offer many things! This includes hiking, riding camels and donkeys, and even visiting Aaron's tomb. You should travel to Jordan, not only to visit Petra but also for the food, the people, and the adventure experiences.
